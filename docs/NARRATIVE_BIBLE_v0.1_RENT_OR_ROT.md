# RUGPULL TYCOON — NARRATIVE BIBLE v0.1

## ACT 1: `RENT OR ROT`

> One basement goblin, one cracked phone, one ex-con uncle, and a rent
> deadline that doesn't care how many candles you mash.

**Status:** docs-only. Nothing here is committed to code yet. This is the
grimy source-of-truth for tone, cast, world, and the first playable loop.

---

## 1. TONE

Cursed dark comedy. 2 AM diner noir. Degen goblin humor.

A grease-stained basement kid mashes fake candles on a cracked phone while a
morally bankrupt uncle feeds him just enough hope to keep him mashing — all
wrapped in arcade shame, trailer-park gags, and the constant threat of
sleeping in a Civic.

Think **GTA radio banter × a cursed idle game that hates you but wants you to
keep playing.**

**Tone rails:**

- Grimy, not explicit. Filthy vibe, clean-ish hands.
- Funny, pathetic, grounded. Every failure is earned and *funny*, never mean.
- The game insults you because it cares. Barely.
- No real scams. No real crypto. No real trading. No wallets. No how-to-crime.
  The "market" is a fake candle toy. It's all vibes and desperation.

---

## 2. CORE STORY PITCH

Dustin "Dust" Mercer is a 23-year-old graveyard-shift burger flipper living
in his mom's damp basement with a rent deadline breathing down his neck.

After another shift at the **Grease Pit**, he ends up at the **Waffle
Mausoleum** at 2 AM, staring at fake charts on his cracked phone. That's when
ex-con grifter **Uncle Sil** slides into the booth and offers to teach him
how the real game works.

What starts as desperate button-mashing to make rent becomes a full
apprenticeship in fake-candle pushing, crew-building, and underworld empire
climbing — until bigger syndicates notice, and the only question left is
whether Dust rugs everyone else, or gets rugged himself.

**Hook:** *Pay rent Friday, or the basement becomes someone else's problem.*

## 3. MAIN CHARACTER — Dustin "Dust" Mercer

- **Age / job:** 23. Graveyard shift at the Grease Pit, flipping mystery
  patties. Sleeps in Mom's basement next to a leaking pipe and a box of old
  action figures.
- **Owns:** A cracked Galaxy phone. Whatever cash is left after rent. Nothing
  else that matters.
- **Personality:** Nervous optimist with a self-deprecating streak. Knows he's
  a loser, keeps whispering *"this next candle is the one."* Quick to panic,
  stubborn once he starts mashing.
- **Shame / fear:** Mom's disappointed sigh. The Civic sleeper in the lot
  becoming his future. Being the family cautionary tale.
- **Motivation:** Pay *this month's* rent so he doesn't get kicked out.
  Everything after that is just momentum he can't stop.
- **Why we root for him:** He's pathetic in a relatable way. Every small win
  feels like a genuine middle finger to the universe.

**Progression titles:**
Basement Pleb → Diner Degenerate → Candle Pusher → Resistance Breaker →
Chart Goblin → Rug Lieutenant → District Operator → Sell Wall Survivor →
Empire Rat → **Final Prestige (Rug Baron / Civic King)**

**Portrait direction:** Gaunt face, permanent dark circles, messy brown hair,
stained grey Grease Pit hoodie, bad posture, clutching the cracked phone like
a lifeline. Ugly-beautiful. Readable at 64 px.

## 4. MENTOR — Uncle Silas "Sil" Varnish

- **Age / vibe:** 58, portly, greasy combover, cheap leather jacket over a
  stained wifebeater, one gold tooth, a constant half-eaten waffle in hand.
- **Criminal rumors (backstory flavor ONLY — never how-to):** Did time for a
  fake "invest in your community garden" scheme. Ran a charity that mostly
  bought him boats. Sold "personal items" on niche sites. Taught fake
  chart-reading seminars out of motel rooms. Once tried to fence scrap metal
  from a construction site that turned out to be someone's actual house.
- **Habits:** Wipes mouth with sleeve. Calls everyone "kid" or "genius."
  Scratches himself when thinking. Always has a "hot tip" that benefits him
  first.
- **How he teaches:** Sits in the booth, points at your screen, explains
  mechanics through insults and diner metaphors. *"That red line? That's the
  jeets waiting to eat your breakfast. Push through or go flip patties."*
- **Why he took interest:** Saw the same hungry, desperate look he had at 25.
  Also needs a fresh face — and someone dumb enough to take the fall.
- **Later arc:** Sil genuinely likes the kid, but will still use him as a
  shield or a cash-out when the rival syndicate makes an offer. The betrayal
  is never cartoonish. It's just business.

**Catchphrases:**
- "The candle don't lie, kid. The chart does."
- "Mash it like rent's due in six hours."

## 5. ANTAGONIST — Ledger "Ledge" Harlan

- **Look:** Tall, slicked-back hair, tailored-but-slightly-shiny suit, always
  holding two phones, cold polite smile.
- **Philosophy:** *"Retail goblins and diner rats are just weather. We control
  the forecast."*
- **Relationship to Sil:** Former cellmate and partner. Sil took the fall on a
  big fake-charity job; Ledge walked away rich and now runs the big leagues.
- **Why he's scary:** Controls networks of fake KOLs, fake audit shops that
  greenlight rugs, and massive sell walls that appear *exactly* when small
  players overextend. Treats everyone below him as livestock.
- **In gameplay:** Sudden massive resistance lines, jeet stampedes he
  "accidentally" triggers, chapter-end taunt screens.

**Taunt:** *"Cute little candle, basement boy. I just put a wall up. Try not
to cry when it eats you."*

## 8. STORY ARC (5 acts)

**Act 1 — `RENT OR ROT` (Basement Pleb).**
Goal: scrape together enough for the first rent chunk. Mechanic: basic SEND
THE CANDLE + resistance lines. Failure: **EVICTED → sleep in the Civic.**
NPCs: Sil, Marge, Mom. Sample mission: *"Break your first resistance line
before Mom calls."*

**Act 2 — Learning the Walls (Diner Apprentice).**
Goal: survive a full shift without overheating. Mechanic: heat management,
overmashing penalties, first Overdrive window. Failure: THUMB DISEASE /
CHART REVERSE. NPCs: Sil + Pawn Pete.

**Act 3 — Building the Crew (Chart Goblin).**
Goal: recruit 3 operators, graduate first coin. Mechanic: operators, candle
chains, graduation. Failure: crew turns / big jeet stampede. NPCs: Mia, Greg,
Ricky.

**Act 4 — Rival Syndicate Notices (Resistance Breaker).**
Goal: survive Ledge's first sell wall, keep the crew alive. Mechanic:
Supercharge, bigger resistance, syndicate taunts. Failure: crew poached / rug
attempt. NPCs: Ledge (first appearance), Jax.

**Act 5 — Empire or Betrayal (Rug Baron).**
Goal: confront Ledge; decide whether to rug Sil or take the fall. Mechanic:
full Overdrive + prestige final choice. Endings: become the new Ledge, go
down with Sil, or disappear into the Civic lot.

## 11. FAILURE STATES (funny & fast)

- **EVICTED** — Rent missed. Basement door slams, stuff hits the curb. Sil:
  *"Tough break. Civic's got good ventilation."* Restart: *"Back to the diner.
  Try not to suck this time."*
- **THUMB DISEASE** — Overmashed. Hand icon cramps and drops the phone.
- **CHART REVERSE** — Overheat. Candle flips red and explodes into confetti of
  lost hope.
- **MOM FOUND THE LEDGER** — Phone discovered. Angry mom silhouette.
- **WAFFLE DEBT DEFAULT** — Too many free waffles, too many bad calls. Marge
  cuts you off.
- **SLEEPING IN THE CIVIC** — Rock bottom. Rain on a broken car window; Car
  Carl waves sadly.
- **RUGGED BY LIFE** — A big syndicate wall. Screen fades to black. Pure
  "thanks for playing" energy.

## 12. DIALOGUE STYLE GUIDE

**Rules:**
- 1–3 lines max. Punchy. No speeches.
- Degen slang: *jeet, rug, candle, mash, wall, heat, breakout, graduate.*
- Mechanics get explained through **insults and diner metaphors**, never
  tutorials.
- Sil = cursed uncle who loves you and will still sell you out.
- Filthy but never pornographic. Grimy, not gross.

**Advice:**
- "The wall ain't gonna break itself. Mash like your stuff's already on the curb."
- "Overdrive's up. Go stupid while the room's looking the other way."
- "Chain it. Momentum's the only thing that doesn't lie in this game."

**Insults:**
- "You mashed like a kid who just discovered elbows. Heat's everywhere now."
- "That wasn't a breakout, that was a polite suggestion. Jeets laughed."
- "My grandma could've held that chain, and she's been dead eight years."

**Ledge taunts:**
- "Nice candle, goblin. I just told my guys to eat it."
- "Still mashing in the booth? Cute. I own the booth."

**Mom:**
- "Friday, Dustin. Or the basement becomes someone else's problem."
- "I can hear that phone from up here. Turn it off and get a real job."

**Marge:**
- "Sil's got that look again. Whatever he's selling, it's already half a rug."

**Failure / success / heat:**
- "Wall ate it. Back to the fryer, genius."
- "Wall's gone! Jeets are screaming."
- "Too much. Whole diner's looking. Sit down."
- "You just turned the chart into a crime scene."

## 13. MECHANICS → STORY MAPPING

| Mechanic | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| SEND THE CANDLE | Shoving your last bit of hope into a fake market |
| Resistance lines | Sell walls — jeets, rivals, or Ledge waiting to eat small players |
| Taps → velocity | Raw desperate effort, shilling, will to survive |

| Candle Chain | Real momentum instead of one desperate pump |
| Supercharge | The room is fully farmed — maximum velocity |
| Overdrive | Rare window where nobody's watching |
| Operators | Your crew handling the dirty parts so you don't burn out |
| Graduation | Exiting a small play into a bigger venue (or rugging it clean) |
| Prestige | Burning it all down and restarting with lessons + better tools |

## 14. CONTENT BOUNDARIES

**Hard nos:** No real scam instructions. No step-by-step fraud. No real wallet
addresses. No real trading mechanics or crypto. No organ/body-harm detail.
No explicit gore or sexual content.

All criminal elements stay **grotesque backstory jokes and world flavor** —
never actionable.

**Fictional replacements:**
- Waffle House → **The Waffle Mausoleum**
- Burger job → **The Grease Pit**
- Weird body-content hustle → *"personal item auctions"* (rumor only)
- Fake guru courses → *"Chart Whisperer seminars"* (backstory only)

The "market" is an arcade candle toy with no relationship to real assets.
